Virtual Hardware Lab

Virtual Hardware Lab is a Virtually Real-time Hardware simulator System. In Lockdown 2020 I got this idea & I started implementing this idea & got funding from Bangladesh ICT Division(Currently I'm working on it).

The knowledge of robotics, IoT, and Automation of this countryâ€™s (Bangladesh) students is very limited compared to the students of other countries. In a survey, it was found that 60% of students who are interested to learn about robotics and IoT do not have access to the right equipment which is why they fail to acquire that skill, the expensive instruments are not easily accessible or affordable to learn Robotics and IOT skills at home. Even the institutional labs do not support enough to pursue this skill. Besides this, the percentage of students who have enough technological knowledge is close to zero. To learn more about Robotics, IoT, and Automation students have to rely on very few research labs like Sheikh Rasel's digital lab which is not enough for the number of students in this country. Other than that, the simulation software is accessible and does not offer real-life sensor simulators, which is why the research aspects are very limited for students.

We are chasing the impact that developing Modern skill sets like programming, STEM, IoT, Robotics, and AI education that are necessary for the career growth of a student can be accessed easily. To keep up with the modern world, it is essential to provide proper equipment and research support to those who are eager to learn about robotics, IoT, and Automation. This is why we have conducted research and surveys to come up with a virtual hardware lab where students from any corner of the region would have access to any component of our virtual lab when needed. Students would be able to get the physical lab experience through online. They would be able to directly program and control devices in the virtual hardware lab. Different kinds of sensors and devices would be provided to run the research. Even projects can be made through this virtual hardware lab.
